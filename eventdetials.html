Evacuation Center Visit & Drill for Foreign Residents – Building Inclusive Resilience

Date: 7 September 2025

Location: Yokohama, Japan

Organised by: REarth (ReadyJapan, run by Women in Climate Leadership)

Collaborators / Partners: Midori Ward Office; Kirigaoka Jichikai (Neighbourhood Association)

Overview

A hands-on evacuation-center visit and disaster-preparedness drill, featuring first formal inclusion of foreign residents in Kirigaoka. Live demos included standpipe fire extinguishers, smoke experience, temporary toilets, and shelter logistics, with real-time English translation to ensure equal access.

Impact & Outcomes

  • First formal inclusion of foreign residents in a Kirigaoka community drill.
  • Improved familiarity with evacuation procedures, safety protocols, and facility use.
  • Reduced language and cultural barriers; stronger trust among residents, authorities, and foreign communities.

Why It Matters

Preparedness succeeds only when it includes everyone. Bridging language and cultural gaps empowers foreign residents as active contributors, advancing inclusive resilience and shared safety.

Heatstroke Awareness Workshop – Building Climate-Resilient Communities

Date: 27 July 2025

Location: Yokohama, Japan

Organised by: ReadyJapan (REarth – DRR Wing)

Collaborators / Partners: CEEW India; Yokohama Mandal

Expert Speaker: Dr. Saru Gupta, Council on Energy, Environment and Water (CEEW)

Overview

Part of REarth’s climate adaptation series addressing rising heatstroke risks amid intensifying heatwaves. The session covered climate–health links, risk signals, household and community adaptation practices, and first-aid responses grounded in science and daily life.

Impact & Outcomes

  • Engaged 20+ participants across age groups.
  • Ignited neighborhood awareness drives and peer-sharing of heat-safety practices.
  • Strengthened ReadyJapan’s community-based DRR by tying adaptation to personal health safety.

Why It Matters

Extreme heat is a rapidly growing climate risk. Using a Head–Heart–Hand approach turned knowledge into empathy and immediate action to protect health in a warming world.
