Rocket Pitch Night – Venture Café Tokyo

Date: 7 May 2025

REarth will be participating in the Rocket Pitch Night at Venture Café Tokyo — one of Japan’s most dynamic gatherings for entrepreneurs and changemakers. Our founder, Dr. Bageshree K., will be presenting REarth’s mission to revolutionize climate education through school-based, action-driven learning. From SDG Star School Programs to climate labs and teacher training, we’re working continuously on turning climate anxiety into climate agency — one school at a time.

Come meet us at Booth #14 to learn how education can be the root of real climate action. We’re looking forward to meaningful conversations, new connections, and exploring ways to collaborate.

ReadyJapan Community Launch Event

Date: 5 May 2025

The ReadyJapan initiative officially kicked off with its first community meeting in Midori-ku, Yokohama. The two-hour interactive session brought together foreign residents to discuss disaster preparedness needs, share personal experiences, and explore how REadyJapan can support inclusive and accessible disaster risk reduction. Participants engaged in group discussions, offered valuable insights, and contributed to shaping the future direction of the program. The event marked the beginning of a community-led effort to bridge the information gap and build resilience among Japan’s diverse population.

Earth Month Climate Workshops 2025 – Shaping Climate-Conscious Futures

Throughout Earth Month 2025, REarth successfully hosted a series of impactful EN-ROADS climate simulation workshops, with over 80 participants registering from diverse backgrounds. These sessions empowered participants to explore real-time climate policy solutions and take action to reduce global temperature rise in line with the Paris Agreement. Many shared powerful emotional shifts—from fear to commitment, and from sadness to renewed hope—demonstrating the transformative power of climate education. Our efforts were also proudly recognized as part of the Worldwide Climate and Justice Education Week 2025.

Future Campus 2050: Climate Action Challenge

REarth collaborated to conduct an engaging student-led challenge as part of an Indian University course on disasters. The activity, rooted in the principle of “Think Global – Act Local,” invited students to reimagine their campuses as sustainable, climate-resilient spaces by the year 2050. The Future Campus 2050 challenge encouraged over 80 students to identify real-world climate issues within their institutions and propose practical, visionary solutions. Through creative presentations, students showcased their ideas with clarity, feasibility, and local relevance. A recurring concern raised by many teams was canteen food waste, which they addressed with thoughtful and actionable strategies. This activity was part of REarth’s standard education module and encouraged youth to think critically and act responsibly for a sustainable planet.

ENROADS Climate Workshop - Tokyo Session

Date: 23 March 2025

REarth successfully conducted an ENROADS Climate Workshop in Tokyo, in partnership with Ichigo Bloom. The session brought together a diverse group of participants to explore the impact of various climate policies and interventions using the ENROADS simulator—developed by Climate Interactive and MIT Sloan. This interactive session allowed participants to step into the shoes of decision-makers, test different climate strategies, and witness their effects on global temperature rise in real time. The workshop sparked dynamic discussions and deep reflection, with many participants sharing that they initially felt disconnected or scared about climate change—but left the session feeling committed, hopeful, and empowered to act.

How to Compost – A Hands-On Workshop by REarth

Date: 25 March 2025

REarth recently hosted an inspiring How to Compost workshop, led by Ms. Oana Marin, a passionate community compost guide. With her practical insights and gentle encouragement, Oana beautifully broke down the nuances of composting, making it accessible and engaging for everyone—from complete beginners to the curious eco-conscious. Participants not only learned different composting methods suitable for homes of all sizes but also discovered how this simple act can contribute to a healthier planet. The session sparked motivation and confidence among attendees to start composting in their own homes, turning everyday waste into a powerful tool for sustainability.
