Community-Led Disaster Preparedness Initiative

Disasters are no longer rare events, and in Japan, the fear of earthquakes is a constant reality. At the same time, climate change is making extreme weather more frequent and intense‚Äîlonger heatwaves, stronger typhoons, and rising temperatures are becoming the new normal. In such uncertain times, adaptation and preparedness are not just important; they are essential.

With the principle of "No one left behind," we are developing a community-led Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) program‚Äîcreated by foreigners, for foreigners. This initiative could be one of the first of its kind, ensuring that international residents are equipped with the knowledge and resources to stay safe.

How You Can Help: Universities & Researchers, DRR Experts, Funding & Sponsorships, Organizations

Community DRR for Foreign Residents

I am excited to share that recently we had our first preliminary meeting of the Indian community members to discuss developing a (Local) Community Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) Plan for non-Japanese speakers of our area in Yokohama, Japan. The need for such a program was unanimously recognized, and many valuable ideas were exchanged.

Living in an earthquake-prone country, foreign residents often struggle with access to critical disaster information, preparedness measures, and communication platforms. Through this initiative, we aim to bridge this gap and create a structured DRR plan tailored to the needs of non-Japanese speakers.

Support Needed: Expert Guidance, Research & Collaboration, Sponsorship & Funding
