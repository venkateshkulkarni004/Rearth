The Challenge

Reducing our carbon footprint may seem daunting, but every step counts. By understanding our individual and collective impact, we can make informed choices that align with global climate goals while benefiting our communities and businesses.

The Approach

The 2 Tonnes workshop is an immersive, role-playing simulation that projects participants into the future, challenging them to collaboratively limit climate change by reducing emissions to 2 tonnes of COâ‚‚ per person per year by 2050. Participants explore various individual and collective actions, experiencing the outcomes of their decisions in a simulated environment.

The Outcome

Attendees gain practical insights into the magnitude of changes required to achieve carbon neutrality. The workshop inspires proactive engagement and equips participants with the knowledge to implement effective carbon reduction strategies within their organizations and communities.

Logistics

  • Duration: 3 hours.
  • Format: Interactive simulation; available for in-person and online participation.
  • Participants: Suitable for individuals, students, businesses, educational institutions, policymakers, and community organizations.
  • Facilitator: The workshop is led by our trained, enthusiastic, and adaptable facilitator, ensuring an engaging and insightful learning experience.

Bring This Workshop to Your Organization

Are you ready to take action and bring meaningful climate education to your team? Hosting this workshop is simple! Here's what you need to do:

  • Express Interest: Fill out our contact form or email us at contact.rearth[at]gmail.com to let us know you're interested.
  • Discuss Your Needs: We will schedule a quick call to understand your goals, audience, and preferred format.
  • Plan & Customize: We tailor the workshop to align with your organization's objectives and schedule.
  • Engage & Learn: Our facilitators will conduct the session, making learning engaging, interactive, and impactful.
  • Take Action: Walk away with insights, strategies, and practical steps to integrate sustainability into your organization.
