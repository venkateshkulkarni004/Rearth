REadyJapan: Disaster Risk Reduction Program for Foreigners in Japan (DRRPF)

REadyJapan is an initiative dedicated to enhancing disaster preparedness and resilience among foreign residents in Japan. Recognizing that language barriers, lack of access to emergency information, and unfamiliarity with local disaster response systems put foreign communities at a greater risk during crises, this program aims to bridge the gap.

What is REadyJapan?

REadyJapan equips foreign residents with the knowledge and resources needed to effectively respond to disasters such as earthquakes, typhoons, and heatwaves. The program ensures a comprehensive approach that includes preparedness, response, recovery, mitigation, and adaptation.

How We Are Structuring the Program

REadyJapan is being developed in collaboration with local communities, ward officials, and disaster risk reduction (DRR) experts to ensure effective implementation. The key components of the program include:

  • Multilingual Disaster Preparedness Workshops – Educating foreign residents on emergency response, evacuation procedures, and risk reduction strategies.
  • Community Evacuation Drills – Conducting hands-on drills to familiarize residents with emergency shelters and protocols.
  • Development of English Resources – Translating hazard maps, evacuation guides, and emergency procedures into multiple languages for wider accessibility.
  • Heatwave and Extreme Weather Preparedness – Providing guidance on heatstroke prevention and typhoon safety.
  • Collaboration with Local Authorities – Strengthening partnerships with ward offices to ensure inclusivity in disaster response plans.

How You Can Help

REadyJapan thrives on community engagement and collaboration. Currently, we are operating in Midori-ku, Yokohama, Japan. Here's how you can help:

  • Volunteer & Community Engagement – Assist in organizing workshops, evacuation drills, and awareness campaigns. Help translate and adapt emergency materials into multiple languages for better accessibility. Support outreach efforts to connect with foreign communities who may lack disaster preparedness resources.
  • Research & Knowledge Sharing – Collaborate on research projects related to disaster resilience, foreign community risk perception, or inclusive DRR strategies. Contribute insights on best practices for disaster preparedness from other countries and how they can be adapted in Japan. Help develop data-driven solutions to improve emergency response mechanisms for non-Japanese speakers.
  • Educational & Capacity-Building Initiatives – Conduct training sessions on earthquake preparedness, climate-induced disasters, and emergency response. Provide expertise in heatwave resilience, mental health during disasters, or digital tools for emergency communication. Develop educational materials tailored for schools, workplaces, and community centers.
  • Institutional & Organizational Partnerships – If you represent a university, NGO, or research institution, partner with us to integrate disaster preparedness education into existing programs. Local businesses & embassies can collaborate by providing funding, venues for training, or logistical support for community drills.

Sponsor and Support

While REadyJapan is driven by passionate individuals, we need financial support for the execution of our impact and to ensure that every foreign resident has access to life-saving information and preparedness training.

Got Another Idea?

If you have expertise, insights, or resources that could contribute to disaster preparedness and resilience, we'd love to hear from you! Your support, big or small, can help save lives by empowering foreign communities with the knowledge and tools to stay safe during disasters.
